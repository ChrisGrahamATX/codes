About Me

As a UX/UI Designer, my expertise lies in creating user-friendly designs that prioritize accessibility. Given my personal experience of being diagnosed with ADHD at a young age, I consistently highlight the significance of inclusivity and neurodiversity in my UX decisions. With a “less is more” approach in mind, I am doing my best to provide the most satisfying user experience via the simplest approaches.

Outside the office, I have been trying to enhance my design skills through creative projects. I am planning to take on a furniture design class soon which I am very excited about! Besides the designer role, I am also a gamer, guitar player, fine artist, a foodie, a hiker, and a traveler. I look forward to sharing more stories with you.
